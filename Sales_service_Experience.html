Introduction to Salesforce & Sales Cloud

Salesforce is the world's #1 CRM platform, delivered as cloud-based SaaS. Founded in 1999 by Marc Benioff, it removed the need for on-premise installations. Salesforce runs on the multi-tenant Force.com platform — all customers share the same infrastructure but have completely isolated data through metadata-based separation.

What is Sales Cloud?

Sales Cloud helps sales teams manage the complete sales cycle — from identifying prospects to closing deals. It provides tools for lead management, opportunity tracking, account and contact management, sales forecasting, and automation to increase pipeline visibility, improve rep productivity, and close more deals faster.

Key Business Benefits

  • 360-degree view of every customer and prospect in a single platform
  • Automated workflows that eliminate repetitive manual tasks for reps
  • Real-time dashboards and forecasting for full management visibility
  • Mobile access — reps can update CRM from any device, anywhere
  • Email integration (Gmail/Outlook) to log activities automatically
  • Einstein AI for predictive lead scoring and opportunity insights
  • AppExchange ecosystem with thousands of pre-built integrations

🔗 Key Relationship: Lead Conversion Flow

When a Lead is qualified and converted, Salesforce creates (or matches) an Account, Contact, and optionally an Opportunity in a single atomic operation. This is the most important relationship flow in Sales Cloud — and a guaranteed interview topic.

Object Relationships

  • Account → Contact: One Account can have many Contacts (Lookup relationship)
  • Account → Opportunity: One Account can have many Opportunities (Lookup)
  • Opportunity → Contact: Many-to-many via Opportunity Contact Role junction object
  • Opportunity → Product: Many-to-many via Opportunity Line Item (OpportunityLineItem)
  • Account → Account: Parent-Child hierarchy via Parent Account field (self-lookup)

Lead Management & Conversion

Leads represent unqualified prospects. Effective lead management ensures reps focus on high-value prospects and no opportunity is missed. The journey from raw lead to qualified opportunity is the foundation of the sales pipeline.

Lead Lifecycle

1
Lead Generation

Generated through web forms, marketing campaigns, trade shows, purchased lists, or manual rep entry

2
Lead Assignment

Assigned to a queue or rep using Lead Assignment Rules (geography, industry, lead source criteria)

3
Auto-Response

Auto-response rules send personalised email acknowledgements based on lead attributes

4
Qualification

Reps make calls, send emails, log activities. Status updated: Open → Working → Nurturing → Qualified

5
Conversion

Qualified leads are converted — creates Account + Contact + Opportunity in one atomic action

Lead Assignment Rules

Lead Assignment Rules automatically route incoming leads to the right owner (user or queue) based on rule criteria. Only ONE rule can be active at a time, but it can contain multiple rule entries evaluated in order.

  • Rule entries evaluated top-to-bottom — first matching entry wins
  • Criteria can include any lead field: Country, Industry, Annual Revenue, Lead Source
  • If no rule matches, lead goes to the default owner specified in the rule
  • Triggered from Web-to-Lead, Data Import Wizard, or by checking "Assign using active assignment rule"

Web-to-Lead

Web-to-Lead generates HTML form code that you embed on your website. When a visitor submits, a Lead record is automatically created in Salesforce.

Configuration PointDetail
Daily Limit500 leads/day by default (can be increased with Salesforce Support)
Spam PreventionreCAPTCHA can be added to the generated form
Auto-ResponseAuto-response rules send confirmation emails to the submitter
AssignmentAssignment rules automatically route the new lead to the correct owner
Return URLThank-you page URL the visitor is redirected to after submission

Lead Conversion — What Actually Happens

⚡ Under the Hood — Lead Conversion

When a rep clicks 'Convert': Salesforce searches for matching Accounts (by company name). Rep chooses to create a new Account or merge into existing. A new Contact is created linked to the Account. Optionally, an Opportunity is created. Lead Field Mapping transfers values from Lead fields to Account/Contact/Opportunity fields. The Lead is marked IsConverted = TRUE and locked. The ConvertedAccountId, ConvertedContactId, and ConvertedOpportunityId are stamped on the Lead record.

Account & Contact Management

Account Hierarchies

Accounts can be organised in parent-child hierarchies using the Parent Account lookup field. This enables enterprise account management where a global corporation has regional subsidiaries all linked together.

  • Hierarchy limited to 10,000 Accounts in a single chain
  • Rollup summary fields cannot cross hierarchy levels (only Master-Detail, not Lookup)
  • Account Teams: Multiple reps assigned roles on one account for collaborative selling

Person Accounts

⚠️ Important — Person Accounts

Person Accounts merge the Account and Contact into a single record for B2C scenarios. Once enabled, Person Accounts cannot be disabled. Used when the customer is an individual rather than a company. Every Account will have either a 'Business Account' or 'Person Account' record type. Contact fields appear directly on the Account record.

Sales Process & Path

A Sales Process defines which Opportunity stages are available for a particular Record Type. Sales Path provides visual guidance to reps about what steps to take at each stage — key fields, tips, checklists right on the record.

Sales Process Configuration Steps

  1. Create a Sales Process — Setup → Sales Processes → New. Select stages from the master stage list
  2. Create a Record Type — Object Manager → Opportunity → Record Types. Assign the Sales Process
  3. Assign Profiles — Specify which profiles have access to each Record Type
  4. Create Page Layout — Assign appropriate layout to each Record Type + Profile combination

Kanban View

Kanban View displays Opportunities as cards in columns organised by Stage. Reps can drag-and-drop cards to update stages. Summary values (sum of Amount) are shown per column. Alerts flag stale records. Available on all list views with filtering support.

💡 Pro Tip — Path Guidance

For each stage in Path, configure up to 5 Key Fields (display critical fields without scrolling) and Guidance for Success (rich text with checklists, links, best practices). This is one of the highest-ROI configurations you can build for a sales team — it eliminates "what should I do next?" questions entirely.

Cases — The Heart of Service Cloud

A Case represents a customer issue, question, complaint, or service request. Every customer interaction that requires tracking and resolution should generate a Case. Cases are linked to Accounts and Contacts and can be linked to Assets, Contracts, Orders, and Knowledge Articles.

Key Case Fields

FieldDescription

SubjectBrief description — the 'title' of the case

OriginHow the case was created: Phone, Email, Web, Chat, Social, Self-Service
IsEscalatedCheckbox — set automatically by Escalation Rules when SLA is breached
IsClosedAuto-set when Status maps to a 'closed' status category
AssetIdLinks case to a specific product/asset the customer owns

Case Creation Channels

ChannelMechanism

Email-to-Case — Configuration Steps

  1. Setup → Feature Settings → Service → Email-to-Case → Enable Email-to-Case
  2. Choose On-Demand Email-to-Case (recommended — uses Salesforce email service)
  3. Create a Routing Address — Salesforce generates a unique forwarding address
  4. Set up email forwarding in your email server to Salesforce's generated address
  5. Configure Case fields populated from email: Subject, Body, From → Contact lookup
  6. Set up Assignment Rules to route cases to the correct queue or agent

Escalation Rules

Escalation Rules automatically escalate cases that haven't been resolved within a time threshold — they set IsEscalated = TRUE and can reassign the case and send notification emails.

  • Time trigger: Hours or Days since case creation OR last modification
  • Business Hours: Escalation time can be calculated against business hours only (ignoring nights/weekends)
  • Actions: Reassign case, send email notification to manager or team
  • One active escalation rule per org with multiple rule entries evaluated top-down

Entitlements & Milestones (SLA Management)

Entitlements define the specific support terms a customer is entitled to receive — essentially the contractual SLA. Milestones within Entitlements define measurable, time-based steps that must be completed to meet the SLA.

ObjectDescription
EntitlementMaster record defining SLA terms. Linked to Account, Contact, or Asset
Entitlement TemplateReusable template to quickly create Entitlements with predefined settings
Service ContractContractual agreement. Entitlements can be linked to Service Contracts
MilestoneTime-bound step in Entitlement Process (e.g., First Response ≤ 1hr)
Entitlement ProcessSequence of Milestones that fires when a Case meets entry criteria
📋 Example — Premium Support SLA

Milestone 1: First Response — respond within 1 Business Hour. Completed when agent posts first reply. Violation: email to Support Manager.

Milestone 2: Resolution — case resolved within 8 Business Hours. Completed when IsClosed = TRUE. Violation: escalate to Director, send apology to customer.

Milestone Key Properties

  • Time Trigger: Minutes after case creation (or previous milestone completion)
  • Success Criteria: Formula determining when milestone is 'completed'
  • Business Hours: Milestone timers run on business hours only — fair SLA measurement
  • Actions: Warning (at risk), Violated (breached), Success (completed) — each triggers different actions
  • Recurrence: No recurrence, Independent (restarts on re-opening), or Sequential

Knowledge Base (Salesforce Knowledge)

Salesforce Knowledge allows organisations to create, manage, publish, and report on articles — solutions, how-to guides, FAQs, and troubleshooting steps. Articles deflect cases (customers self-serve) and help agents resolve cases faster with the right information at their fingertips.

Article Lifecycle

1
Draft

Article created and being authored. Not visible to agents or customers

2
Review

Submitted for editorial review. Awaiting approval via Approval Process

3
Published

Article is live — visible to agents and/or customers based on Data Category visibility settings

4
Archived

No longer current. Hidden from views but retained for audit purposes

Data Categories

Data Categories provide a hierarchical classification for Knowledge Articles, controlling which articles are visible to which users. Structure: Category Group → Categories → Sub-Categories

  • A Category Group is a top-level container (e.g., Products, Regions, Topics)
  • Each group has a hierarchy of values (e.g., Products → Hardware → Laptops)
  • Articles are tagged with one or more categories during authoring
  • Visibility controlled by assigning Data Category access to Profiles (internal) or Community members (external)

Security, Sharing & Visibility

Security in Experience Cloud is multi-layered. External users access a subset of Salesforce data through carefully configured sharing and visibility controls. Both over-sharing (data leak) and under-sharing (broken portal) are failures.

⚠️ Guest User Security — Critical

Never give Guest Users more access than absolutely necessary. Salesforce enforces strict OWD for Guest Users — they can only access records where OWD is Public Read Only or higher. Always use 'Secure Guest User Record Access' (enabled by default in recent orgs). Over-permissioning Guest Users is a major security vulnerability.

Sharing RulesCriteria-based rules for Customer Community Plus and Partner licenses

Manual SharingRecord-level sharing done by owner or admin

Quick Reference — Key Differences

Sales Cloud

Lead vs Contact
Lead = unqualified prospect (no Account required). Contact = qualified person linked to an Account.
Lookup vs Master-Detail
Lookup: optional parent, no rollup. Master-Detail: required parent, enables rollup summary, cascade delete.
WFR vs Flow
WFR/Process Builder = legacy (being retired). Always use Flow for new automations.
Profiles vs Roles
Profiles = what you CAN DO (access). Roles = what you CAN SEE (data visibility via hierarchy).

Service Cloud

Email-to-Case vs On-Demand
On-Demand: Salesforce email service, no firewall changes. Standard: requires local email agent on your server.
Entitlement vs Milestone
Entitlement = overall SLA contract. Milestone = individual time-bound step within the SLA.
Live Agent vs Messaging
Live Agent = synchronous (both online). Messaging = asynchronous (conversation persists over time).
Macro vs Quick Text
Macro: series of agent ACTIONS in one click. Quick Text: pre-written MESSAGE snippet inserted with a shortcut.

Experience Cloud

Customer vs Partner License
Customer: B2C, limited object access. Partner: B2B, full CRM access for deal/lead management.
Sharing Sets
Used for Customer Community license ONLY. Share records based on field relationships (AccountId match).
Aura vs LWR
Aura: mature, more components. LWR: faster, better SEO, headless-ready — use for new sites.
Guest User Security
Minimum access only. Never grant Create/Edit/Delete to sensitive objects. Secure Guest User Record Access must be enabled.
